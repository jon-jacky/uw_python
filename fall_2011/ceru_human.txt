CC   -!- FUNCTION: CERULOPLASMIN IS A BLUE, COPPER-BINDING (6-7 ATOMS PER
CC       MOLECULE) GLYCOPROTEIN FOUND IN PLASMA. FOUR POSSIBLE FUNCTIONS
CC       ARE FERROXIDASE ACTIVITY, AMINE OXIDASE ACTIVITY, COPPER TRANSPORT
CC       AND HOMEOSTASIS, AND SUPEROXIDE DISMUTASE ACTIVITY.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 4 FE(2+) + 4 H(+) + O(2) = 4 FE(3+) + 2 H(2)O.
CC   -!- COFACTOR: BINDS 6 CU-IONS PER MOLECULE. THIS PROTEIN BELONGS TO
CC       THE MULTICOPPER OXIDASES WHICH CONTAIN THREE DISTINCT CU CENTERS
CC       KNOWN AS TYPE 1 OR BLUE, TYPE 2 OR NORMAL, AND TYPE 3 OR COUPLED
CC       BINUCLEAR.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: SYNTHESIZED IN LIVER AND SECRETED INTO THE
CC       PLASMA.
CC   -!- DISEASE: CERULOPLASMIN IS DEFICIENT IN WILSON'S DISEASE.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: CONTAINS 3 F5/8 TYPE A DOMAINS; EACH IS COMPOSED OF
CC       2 PLASTOCYANIN-LIKE REPEATS.
